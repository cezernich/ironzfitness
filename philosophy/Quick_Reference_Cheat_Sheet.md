# Strength Training Research - Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

## Volume Landmarks (MEV → MAV → MRV)

| Muscle Group | MEV | MAV (Sweet Spot) | MRV |
|---|---|---|---|
| Large (Chest, Back, Legs) | 6-8 sets/week | 12-18 sets/week | 20-25+ sets/week |
| Small (Biceps, Triceps, Calves) | 4-6 sets/week | 8-12 sets/week | 15-20 sets/week |

**Experience Adjustment:**
- Beginners: 10-12 sets/week per muscle
- Intermediate: 12-18 sets/week per muscle
- Advanced: 18-20+ sets/week per muscle

---

## Periodization Models - The Verdict

| Model | Best For | Hypertrophy | Strength | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Linear** | Beginners | ✓ | ✓✓ | Simplest, works well initially |
| **Undulating** | Intermediate | ✓✓ | ✓✓ | Best variety without complexity |
| **Block** | Advanced | ✓✓ | ✓✓✓ | Maximum specialization |

**Bottom Line:** Choose one you'll actually do. All work equally if volume is matched.

## Progressive Overload Methods

| Method | Best For | Ease | Sustainability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Linear** (add weight each week) | Beginners | Easy | 6-12 months |
| **Double Progression** | All | Moderate | Long-term |
| **Autoregulated (RIR-based)** | Experienced | Moderate | Long-term |
| **RPE-Based** | Experienced | Hard | Long-term |
| **Percentage-Based** | Powerlifters | Hard | Long-term |

**Recommendation:** Start linear, shift to double progression for longevity.

## Training Frequency

**The Evidence:** Training 2x/week per muscle = 63% better hypertrophy than 1x/week

| Frequency | Effect | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| 1x/week | Baseline hypertrophy | Limited |
| 2x/week | +63% better hypertrophy | **Optimal minimum** |
| 3x/week | Similar to 2x/week (volume-matched) | Advanced only |

**Bottom Line:** Train each muscle **2x per week minimum**

---

## Rep Range Research - The Myth Busted

**Finding:** 17 of 20 studies = NO significant difference in hypertrophy between rep ranges when volume is equal.

| Rep Range | Purpose | When to Use | Volume Needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-5 reps | Strength | Heavy compounds | 30-40 weekly reps |
| 6-10 reps | **Hypertrophy** | **Primary training** | 40-60 weekly reps |
| 12-20+ reps | Hypertrophy + Pump | Isolation & volume | 60-100+ weekly reps |

**Bottom Line:** Use 40% at 6-10 reps (most efficient), 30% at 3-5, 30% at 12-20+

---

## Rest Periods Between Sets

| Goal | Rep Range | Optimal Rest |
|---|---|---|
| Strength | 1-5 reps | **3-5 minutes** |
| Hypertrophy | 6-10 reps | **2-3 minutes** (updated 2024) |
| Hypertrophy | 12-15 reps | 60-120 seconds |
| Endurance | 15-20+ reps | 30-60 seconds |

**Key Update:** 2024 meta-analysis suggests longer rests may be better for hypertrophy than traditionally recommended (60-90s).

## Training Splits Comparison

| Split | Frequency | Time | Best For | Frequency/Muscle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Full-Body** | 3x/week | 45 min | Beginners | 3x/week |
| **Upper/Lower** | 4x/week | 60 min | Intermediate | 2x/week |
| **Push/Pull/Legs** | 6x/week | 75 min | Advanced | 2x/week |

**Finding:** No difference in hypertrophy when frequency per muscle is equal. Choose by preference.

## Proximity to Failure

**Evidence:** Training at 1-3 RIR = same hypertrophy as training to failure, with better recovery

| Goal | Recommended RPE | Reps in Reserve | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hypertrophy | 7-8 | 2-3 RIR | Most sets at this level |
| Hypertrophy | 8-9 | 1-2 RIR | Final set okay |
| Strength | 8-9 | 1-2 RIR | Avoid failure on heavy |
| Isolation | 8-10 | 0-2 RIR | Can push harder |

**Bottom Line:** You DON'T need to train to failure. 1-3 RIR is optimal.

---

## Deload Protocol

**When:** Every 6-12 weeks (proactive) OR when 2-3 overtraining signs appear (reactive)

**Signs of Overtraining:**
- Persistent form degradation
- Strength decreases despite harder effort
- Elevated resting HR (>10 bpm above baseline)
- Persistent fatigue/mood changes
- Sleep disruption

**Deload Structure (1 week):**
- Reduce volume to 40-60% of normal (6-9 sets instead of 15)
- Maintain movement quality
- Keep compounds, but lighter
- Train at RPE 6-7 (3-4 RIR)
- Focus on recovery: sleep, nutrition, stress management

## Compound vs. Isolation

| Type | Hypertrophy | Efficiency | Use | Mix |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Compound** | ✓ Equal | ✓✓ (Best) | 60-70% of training | High |
| **Isolation** | ✓ Equal | ✓ (Good) | 30-40% of training | Supplementary |

**Bottom Line:** Build program around compounds; use isolation for weak points or pump work.

## Volume per Exercise per Week

**General Guideline:** 30-60 weekly reps per exercise to drive hypertrophy

| Exercise Type | Weekly Volume Range | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Main Compound | 30-50 reps | 5 sets × 6-8 reps twice/week = 50-80 reps |
| Secondary Compound | 40-60 reps | 4 sets × 8-10 reps twice/week = 64 reps |
| Isolation | 40-80 reps | 3 sets × 12 reps twice/week = 72 reps |

## Andy Galpin's Framework (Simplified)

**The 3-5 Concept:**
- 3-5 **exercises**
- 3-5 **rep range** (if using variable reps)
- 3-5 **sets per exercise**
- 3-5 **minute rests** (between compounds)
- 3-5 **times per week**

**Hypertrophy Targets:**
- Minimum: 10 working sets per muscle/week
- Optimal: 15-20 sets per muscle/week (well-trained)
- Rep range: 5-30 reps (very broad flexibility)
- Frequency: 2-3x per week per muscle

## Layne Norton's PHAT Summary

**Daily Undulating Periodization:**
- Day 1: Upper Power (3-5 reps)
- Day 2: Lower Power (3-5 reps)
- Day 3: Rest
- Day 4: Upper Hypertrophy (8-12 reps)
- Day 5: Lower Hypertrophy (8-12 reps)
- Days 6-7: Rest

**Deload:** Every 6-12 weeks at 60-70% intensity for 1-3 weeks

## Autoregulation Methods - Ranked by Effectiveness

1. **APRE** (Autoregulating Progressive Resistance Exercise) - 93% optimal
2. **RPE** (Rating of Perceived Exertion) - 67% optimal
3. **VBRT** (Velocity-Based Training) - 27% optimal
4. **Percentage-Based** - 13% optimal

**Practical:** RPE/RIR methods are most accessible and nearly as effective as complex systems.

## The Research Hierarchy of Importance

**In Priority Order:**

1. **Consistency** - Doing average training consistently beats perfect training inconsistently
2. **Volume** - 10-20 sets per muscle per week (experience-adjusted)
3. **Progressive Overload** - Week-to-week progression in load/reps/RPE
4. **Frequency** - 2x per week per muscle minimum
5. **Exercise Selection** - 60% compound, 40% isolation
6. **Rep Range** - Use variety; 6-10 reps most efficient
7. **Proximity to Failure** - 1-3 RIR for most sets
8. **Rest Periods** - Strength: 3-5 min, Hypertrophy: 2-3 min
9. **Split Structure** - Choose by preference/time
10. **Periodization Model** - All work equally if executed

## Key Takeaways

✓ **Do This:**
- Train consistently (most important)
- Hit 10-20 sets per muscle per week
- Train each muscle 2x/week minimum
- Progress load/reps/RPE week to week
- Keep compounds at 60-70% of training
- Train at 1-3 RIR most of the time
- Use full rep ranges (5-20 reps)
- Log your workouts
- Deload every 6-12 weeks

✗ **Don't Worry About:**
- Whether periodization is linear vs. undulating vs. block
- Specific rep range (6-12 is just most time-efficient)
- Which split (any works if frequency is equal)
- Training to failure (1-3 RIR is better)
- Short vs. long rest periods (both work for hypertrophy)

## Quick Math: Calculating Your Volume

**Example - Chest Training 2x/week:**

If aiming for 14-16 sets/week:
- Day 1: 4 sets bench press + 3 sets incline = 7 sets
- Day 2: 4 sets dumbbell press + 3 sets flyes = 7 sets
- **Total: 14 sets**

Or:
- Day 1: 5 sets bench press = 5 sets
- Day 2: 5 sets decline + 4 sets chest flyes = 9 sets
- **Total: 14 sets**

Both approaches work equally well per the research.
